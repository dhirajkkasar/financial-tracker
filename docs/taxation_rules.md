# India Capital Gains Taxation Summary (FY 2025–26)

## 1. Asset-wise Capital Gains Tax Rules

### 1.1 Equity Shares / Equity Mutual Funds / Equity ETFs
- **Short-Term (≤12 months)**
  - Tax Rate: 20%
- **Long-Term (>12 months)**
  - Tax Rate: 12.5%
  - Exemption: ₹1.25 lakh per year
- **Indexation**: Not allowed
- **Conditions**: STT must be paid

---

### 1.2 Debt Mutual Funds

#### Investments BEFORE April 1, 2023
- ≤24 months: Taxed at slab rate (STCG)
- >24 months: LTCG taxed (earlier with indexation benefits)

#### Investments AFTER April 1, 2023
- Any holding period: Taxed at slab rate
- No LTCG benefit
- No indexation

---

### 1.3 Gold ETFs / International Funds / FoFs
(Treated similar to debt funds)

- ≤24 months: Taxed at slab rate
- >24 months: LTCG taxed at ~12.5%
- Indexation: Not applicable (current regime)

---

### 1.4 Physical Gold
- ≤24 months: Taxed at slab rate
- >24 months: LTCG taxed at 12.5%
- Older regime allowed indexation

---

### 1.5 Foreign Equities (Direct Stocks Abroad)
- ≤24 months: Taxed at slab rate
- >24 months: LTCG taxed at 12.5%
- No exemption threshold

---

### 1.6 Real Estate (Property)
- ≤24 months: Taxed at slab rate
- >24 months:
  - Option 1: 12.5% (no indexation)
  - Option 2: 20% (with indexation under older regime)

---

## 2. Capital Loss Set-Off Rules

### 2.1 Allowed Set-Off

| Loss Type | Can Offset Against |
|----------|-------------------|
| STCL     | STCG + LTCG (any asset class) |
| LTCL     | LTCG only |

### 2.2 Additional Rules
- Loss must be **realized** (asset sold)
- Losses can be **carried forward for 8 years**
- Set-off allowed **across asset classes**

---

## 3. Impact of April 1, 2023 Rule Change

| Asset Class | Before Apr 1, 2023 | After Apr 1, 2023 |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Debt Mutual Funds | LTCG + indexation | Taxed at slab rate |
| Gold / Intl Funds | Indexation available | Mostly removed |
| Equity | Same structure | Rates increased |

### Key Impact:
- Debt mutual funds lost tax efficiency
- Shift towards equity for tax optimization

---

## 4. Unified Tax Table

| Asset | STCG | LTCG | Exemption | Notes |
|------|------|------|----------|------|
| Equity (Shares/MF/ETF) | 20% | 12.5% | ₹1.25L | Most tax-efficient |
| Debt MF (Post-2023) | Slab | Slab | None | No LTCG benefit |
| Debt MF (Pre-2023) | Slab | LTCG (indexed earlier) | None | Legacy benefit |
| Gold ETF / Intl Funds | Slab | 12.5% | None | Treated like debt |
| Physical Gold | Slab | 12.5% | None | Similar to debt |
| Foreign Stocks | Slab | 12.5% | None | No exemption |
| Real Estate | Slab | 12.5% / 20% indexed | None | Dual regime |

---

## 5. Key Insights

- Equity assets are most tax-efficient due to exemption + lower LTCG
- Debt funds lost LTCG/indexation benefits post-2023
- Gold and international funds are taxed like debt
- STCL is flexible (can offset all gains)
- LTCL is restrictive (only LTCG)
- Tax optimization strategies rely heavily on loss harvesting

---

## 6. Practical Tax Optimization Logic

- Use STCL to offset both STCG and LTCG
- Use LTCL only for LTCG planning
- Prioritize booking losses strategically before year-end
- Prefer equity instruments for long-term tax efficiency
